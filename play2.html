Hamlet

Act 1, Scene 3 - Continuation

LAERTES

Then weigh what loss your honour may sustain, If with too credent ear you list his songs, Or lose your heart, or your chaste treasure open To his unmaster'd importunity.

OPHELIA

I shall the effect of this good lesson keep, As watchman to my heart. But, good my brother, Do not, as some ungracious pastors do, Show me the steep and thorny way to heaven;

Lord POLONIUS

Give thy thoughts no tongue, Nor any unproportioned thought his act. Be thou familiar, but by no means vulgar.
